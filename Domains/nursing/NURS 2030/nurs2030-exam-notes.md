# NURS 2030 — Exam Notes

## Module 4 Notes
**Domain 4: Scholarship for the Nursing Discipline**

### Subdomains
- 4.1 Advance the scholarship of nursing
- 4.2 Integrate best evidence into nursing practice
- 4.3 Promote the ethical conduct of scholarly activities

### Research vs. Evidence-Based Practice
- **Research** — generates novel evidence through systematic investigation; experiments to discover new treatments, interventions, or best practices
- **Evidence-Based Practice (EBP)** — integrates current evidence + clinical expertise + individual/family preferences (uses existing knowledge rather than generating new knowledge)

### Essential Elements of EBP
1. **Spirit of Inquiry** — questioning current practice
2. **Identify a Problem** — recognize gaps in practice or healthcare delivery
3. **Search for evidence** — locate quality, credible sources
4. **Evaluate findings** — critically appraise the evidence
5. **Implement recommendations** — apply evidence-based changes
6. **Assess outcomes** — review effectiveness of implementation
7. **Disseminate results** — share findings and outcomes with others

### PICOT Question Framework
Used to identify a clinical problem for EBP:
- **P** — Population: who is the target group?
- **I** — Intervention: what is being tested/done?
- **C** — Comparison: what is the alternative or control?
- **O** — Outcome: what is the desired result?
- **T** — Time: what is the timeframe?

**Example:** For nursing students in 2-year BSN programs (P), what is the importance of standardized exams (I) versus no standardized exams (C) to measure learning and improve test-taking skills (O) each semester (T)?

### Peer-Reviewed vs. Non-Peer-Reviewed Sources
| Type | Characteristics |
|------|----------------|
| Peer-Reviewed | Reviewed by professionals in the field; feedback given; demonstrates rigor and expertise |
| Non-Peer-Reviewed | Anecdotal info and social opinions; lacks rigor; less reliable |

**Questions to evaluate non-peer-reviewed sources:**
- Who conducted the research?
- Are they credentialed?
- Are there biases present?

### Scholarly Databases for EBP
- Medline, AltHealthWatch, Academic Search Premier, CINAHL, Health Source, PsycINFO, ERIC
- Reliable websites: `.edu`, `.gov`, `.org`

## Module 5 Notes
**Domain 5: Quality and Safety**

### Quality Improvement (QI) in Health Care
1. Planning changes in processes or systems to improve client outcomes, efficacy, and clinical performance
2. Identifying and correcting problems by:
   - Minimizing the chance of human error
   - Standardizing processes
   - Utilizing data-driven decision making

### Categories of Quality Measurements
- **Structure** — condition or environment in which care is provided (organizational characteristics, where/context care is delivered)
- **Process** — measures the mechanisms of care provided (how care is delivered to patients)
- **Outcome** — measurable results (effect on patient population; the "why")

### QI Models
- **Plan-Do-Study-Act (PDSA)**
  - Plan: Identify need for change; develop strategic plan
  - Do: Execute the steps in the plan
  - Study: Review results and summarize findings
  - Act: Adapt, abandon, or make revisions; repeat cycle
- **Six Sigma** — reduce defects and variation
- **Lean** — eliminate waste, improve efficiency

### Risk Management
- Identification, evaluation, and prioritization of risks to eliminate or mitigate their probability or severity
- Goal: protect clients, staff, and visitors from harm

### Occurrence / Incident Reports
- Also known as an **adverse event**
- An event NOT consistent with the desired or normal operation of care

### Sentinel Events vs. Never Events
- **Sentinel Event** (Joint Commission definition): "A patient safety event that reaches the patient and results in death, permanent harm, or severe temporary harm and intervention to sustain life."
- **Never Event**: An event that should NEVER occur in a health care setting

### NQF Never Event Categories + Examples
| Category | Example |
|----------|---------|
| Surgical/Procedural | Wrong procedure or wrong site surgery |
| Product/Device | Client death from contaminated instrument |
| Client Protection | Client suicide while receiving inpatient care |
| Care Management | Stage 3/4/unstageable pressure injury acquired in hospital |
| Environmental | Death/injury related to use of restraints |
| Radiologic | Death/injury from metal object in MRI |
| Criminal | Sexual abuse/assault while receiving care in facility |

## Module 7 Notes
**Domain 7: Systems-Based Practice**

### Subdomains
- 7.1 Apply knowledge of systems to work effectively across the continuum of care
- 7.2 Incorporate consideration of cost-effectiveness of care
- 7.3 Optimize system effectiveness through innovation and evidence-based practice

### State Regulation
- Healthcare orgs employ managers/directors to ensure compliance with state regulations via policies and procedures
- States regulate practitioners through:
  - **Nurse Practice Acts**
  - **Standards of Nursing Practice**

### Accreditation
- Performed by outside organizations to assure quality of services and safety of clients
- **The Joint Commission (TJC)**:
  - Surveys documentation, policies, and interviews with administration and staff
  - Provides guidance on how to change and improve practices
  - Full accreditation granted for **3 years**

### Chain of Command (AUCON)
Organizational hierarchy for lines of authority:
- Dean (Gregg Newschwander)
- Associate Dean (Caralise Hunt)
- Course Leader / Student Success Coach → Academic Issues / Progression Problems
- Academic Advisor
- Clinical Course Leader → Clinical Issues
- Clinical Instructor

### Medicare
**Eligibility:**
- Age 65 or older
- Younger than 65 with disabilities
- Any age with end-stage renal disease (ESRD)

**Parts:**
- **Part A** — Hospitalization, skilled nursing facilities, some hospice and home health
- **Part B** — Doctor's services, physical therapy, occupational therapy, some home health
- **Part D** — Prescription drugs

## Exam 3 Notes
**Domain 6: Interprofessional Partnerships** (Exam 3, Module content)

### Collaborative Health Care
- Client-centered approach where members of different health care professions work toward a common goal of improving or restoring a client's health

### Interprofessional (IP) Team
**Benefits of 2+ professionals working together:**
- Improved access to and coordination of health care services
- Greater efficiency of client referral and care services
- Increased quality of community health services
- Decreased complications, length of stay, sentinel events, mortality, and staff turnover

**IP Team members:** Physician, Nurse, Case Manager, Dietitian, Patient, Hospital, Laboratory, Radiology, Pharmacy, Physical Therapist

### IPEC Core Competencies
**Interprofessional Education Collaborative (IPEC):**
- **Values and Ethics** — maintain shared values, ethical conduct, and mutual respect among coworkers
- **Roles and Responsibilities** — use knowledge of each team member's expertise to address health outcomes; know your role and delegate appropriately
- **Communication** — communicate in a responsive, responsible, respectful, and compassionate manner
- **Teams and Teamwork** — apply principles of teamwork science; adapt in various settings

### When to Use the IP Team
- **Rounding** — whole team walks through the unit to check on patients
- **Consultation** — one provider formally requests another's input/advice; original physician still manages the patient
- **Referral** — provider delegates responsibility of managing the client's condition to another provider entirely
- **Discharge Planning** — starts the day the patient is admitted; whole team works together to figure out what the patient needs to go home
  - Use **teach-back** to ensure patient and family understand discharge instructions
  - Encourage client to ask questions
  - Instruct on: medications, dietary/activity limits, complications to report to HCP, prescribed treatments/equipment, follow-up schedule

### IP Team Members
| Role | Key Responsibilities |
|------|---------------------|
| APRN / Nurse Practitioner (NP) | Can diagnose, prescribe meds, and manage patient care independently or alongside physicians |
| Case Manager | Helps with discharge planning, insurance, connecting patients to resources |
| Dietitian | Manages nutritional needs, especially for diabetes, eating disorders, kidney disease, post-surgery |
| Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN) | Works under the RN; cares for stable patients; does dressing changes, suctioning, catheterization, gives medications |

## Exam 2 Notes
**NURS 2030 Exam 2 Study Guide**

### Healthcare-Associated Infections (HAIs)
- More than **500 people per year** acquire healthcare-related infectious deaths
- **SSI** = Surgical Site Infection (a type of healthcare-acquired infection)

### Restraints
- Used to prevent patients from removing IVs, catheters, or other medical equipment
- High-risk intervention — requires monitoring and documentation

### Client Safety Event
- An **unexpected event**, with or without injury, that had the **potential to cause harm**

### Quality Measurements — Process
- **Process** = the mechanism of care provided to patients (one of the 3 categories: Structure, Process, Outcome)
