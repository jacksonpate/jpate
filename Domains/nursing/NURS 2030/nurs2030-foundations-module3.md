# Domain 3 — Population Health (Module 3 PPT Notes)

## What is a Population?

A group of individuals who share one or more personal or environmental characteristics.

## What is Population Health?

The health outcomes of a group of individuals, including:
- The distribution of outcomes within the group
- The factors that affect those outcomes
- The policies and interventions that address those factors

Nurses care for both individual patients AND populations.

---

## Cultural Awareness & Diversity

### Cultural Awareness
Recognizing that cultural differences exist and affect health, beliefs, and behaviors.

### Dimensions of Cultural Diversity

| Dimension | Examples |
|-----------|---------|
| **Age** | Generational differences in beliefs, health behaviors |
| **Gender** | Gender roles, health-seeking behaviors |
| **Ethnicity** | Cultural traditions, practices, values |
| **Gender Identity** | LGBTQ+ considerations in care |
| **Geographic Location** | Rural vs. urban, regional health differences |
| **Language** | Primary language, health literacy in non-English speakers |
| **Religious Beliefs** | Faith-based health practices, restrictions (blood transfusions, dietary laws) |
| **Socioeconomic Status (SES)** | Income, education, employment affecting access to care |

---

## Cultural Competence

The ability to understand, communicate with, and effectively interact with people across cultures.

### Two Key Perspectives

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Emic** | Insider perspective — how a member of the culture sees their own beliefs/practices |
| **Etic** | Outsider viewpoint — how someone from outside the culture observes/interprets the culture |

**Clinical application:** Nurses must strive for emic understanding — meeting patients within their own cultural framework, not imposing the outsider (etic) view.

---

## Health Disparities

Differences in health outcomes that are closely linked with social, economic, and environmental disadvantages.

- Affect racial and ethnic minorities, low-income groups, rural populations, LGBTQ+ individuals
- Examples: higher rates of diabetes, hypertension, cancer mortality in underserved populations
- Nurses must recognize and work to reduce health disparities

---

## Social Determinants of Health (SDOH)

Conditions in the environment where people are born, live, learn, work, play, worship, and age that affect health outcomes.

### 5 SDOH Domains

| Domain | Examples |
|--------|---------|
| **1. Economic Stability** | Employment, income, poverty, food security, housing stability |
| **2. Education Access & Quality** | Literacy, language, early childhood education, higher education |
| **3. Health Care Access & Quality** | Access to primary care, insurance coverage, health literacy |
| **4. Neighborhood & Built Environment** | Housing quality, air/water quality, crime, transportation, parks |
| **5. Social & Community Context** | Social cohesion, discrimination, civic participation, support systems |

---

## Factors Affecting Health

### Demographics
- Age, sex, race/ethnicity, marital status, family structure

### Health Status
- Chronic conditions, disability, functional limitations
- Prior health behaviors (smoking, diet, exercise)

### Socioeconomic Factors
- Education level
- Income and employment
- Access to healthy food
- Housing stability
- Insurance status

---

## Role of the Nurse in Diverse Populations

### Key Responsibilities

1. **Interpreters** — Use professional medical interpreters (not family members) when language barrier exists
2. **Cultural Assessment Tools** — Use validated tools to assess cultural needs (e.g., RESPECT model, LEARN model)
3. **Self-Assessment** — Recognize personal biases and assumptions; practice cultural humility
4. **Public Health Nurse Role** — Focus on population-level interventions, community outreach, health promotion

---

## Client Education

### Why Client Education Matters
- Promotes self-management and adherence
- Reduces readmission rates
- Empowers patients to participate in their care
- Required nursing responsibility per ANA standards

---

## Basic Principles of Adult Learning (6)

Based on Knowles' Adult Learning Theory (Andragogy):

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Relevance** | Learning must be relevant to the learner's life and goals |
| **Self-Directed** | Adults prefer to guide their own learning |
| **Life Experience** | Adults connect new information to prior experience |
| **Readiness** | Learning occurs when the person is ready (emotionally, physically, cognitively) |
| **Task-Centered** | Adults learn best when focused on solving real problems |
| **Motivation** | Internal motivation is stronger than external motivation |

---

## Factors That Affect Learning

| Factor | Considerations |
|--------|---------------|
| **Health Literacy** | Ability to obtain, process, and understand basic health information; use plain language (6th grade reading level) |
| **Basic Needs** | If Maslow's basic needs are unmet (pain, hunger, fear), learning is impaired |
| **Age Diversity** | Teaching approach must match developmental stage (pediatric vs. adult vs. elderly) |
| **Cultural Diversity** | Consider beliefs, values, language, traditional practices in teaching |
| **Reading Levels** | Use appropriate materials; avoid medical jargon; use visuals when helpful |

---

## Teaching Methods

- One-on-one instruction
- Group classes
- Printed materials (at appropriate reading level)
- Videos / multimedia
- Return demonstration (for skill-based teaching)
- Teach-back method — patient explains information back to nurse to confirm understanding

# NURS 2030 Exam 2 — Study Notes (Handwritten)

## Infection Control & Safety

- **>500,000** people acquire healthcare-related infections per year
  - Healthcare-associated infections (HAIs) are a major patient safety concern

- **SSI = Surgical Site Infection**
  - Infection that occurs after surgery at the site of the incision
  - Prevention: sterile technique, hand hygiene, prophylactic antibiotics, skin prep

---

## Restraints

- **Purpose:** Prevent patients from removing IVs, catheters, or other medical equipment
- Least restrictive option must always be tried first
- Requires physician order
- Must be reassessed regularly (every 2 hours per policy)
- Must document: reason, type used, assessment, patient response, alternatives tried

---

## Client Safety Event

- **Definition:** An unexpected event that occurred during care, with or without injury, that had the potential to cause harm to the patient
- Includes: near misses, adverse events, sentinel events

---

## Process Measures (Quality Measurements)

- **Process** = the mechanism of care provided to patients
- Measures whether care was given according to evidence-based guidelines
- Example: Was aspirin given within 24 hours of MI? Was a fall risk assessment completed?
- Contrasted with:
  - **Outcome measures** = what happened to the patient (mortality, readmission, infection rates)
  - **Structure measures** = system resources (staffing ratios, equipment availability)
