## Module 2 — Person-Centered Care

### 2.1 Concepts Related to Patient-Centered Care

**Caring** — the act of nurturing another person to whom one feels commitment or responsibility; holistic in nature.

#### Swanson's Theory of Caring (5 Processes)
1. **Maintaining Belief** — assisting the patient to find meaning in life and maintain a positive attitude
2. **Knowing** — nurse's awareness of assessment data, influencing factors, and the client's own perception of the situation
3. **Being With** — being physically and emotionally present with another person
4. **Doing For** — performance of tasks/activities or an attitude of service
5. **Enabling** — guiding the client and family through situations and events

#### Caring Behaviors
- Listening
- Touch
- Being Present
- Providing Comfort
- Showing Compassion

### Client Preferences
- Client participates as a full member of the healthcare team
- Promoting independence through:
  - Endorsing participation
  - Promoting client understanding
  - Sharing information

### Compassionate Care
- Literally means "to suffer with"
- "Attempts to address the suffering and needs of a person through relational understanding and action"

### Sympathy vs. Empathy
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Sympathy** | Emotional reaction of pity toward the misfortune of another |
| **Empathy (Cognitive)** | Detached acknowledgement and understanding of a distressing situation based on a sense of duty |
| **Empathy (Affective)** | Acknowledgement and understanding by "feeling with" the person |

## Module 3 — Population Health

### Population Health Defined
- **Population** — discrete group the nurse cares for across settings at local, regional, national, and global levels
- **Population health** — "the health outcomes of a group of individuals, including the distribution of such outcomes within the group"
  - Spans the healthcare delivery continuum
  - Focuses on health promotion and illness prevention

### Cultural Awareness and Competence
- **Culture** — integration of human behaviors including communication, language, actions, beliefs, values, customs, and institutions of racial, religious, social, and ethnic groups

#### Cultural Awareness
- More than just knowing other cultures exist
- Requires the ability and willingness to investigate and understand

#### Cultural Diversity Dimensions
- Age, Gender, Ethnicity, Gender identity, Geographic location, Language, Religious beliefs, Socioeconomic status

#### Cultural Competence
- Appreciating, accepting, and respecting individual cultural influences, beliefs, customs, and values
- **Emic knowledge** — a cultural insider's perspective on culture
- **Etic knowledge** — an outsider's viewpoint on culture

### Health Disparities
- Health outcomes that may be greater or lesser among different populations compared to the majority/dominant population
- Disparities tied to access to care for vulnerable populations

### Social Determinants of Health (SDOH)
- "The conditions in the environments where people are born and live that affect their health, well-being, and quality of life."
- Key SDOH factors:
  - Demographics
  - Socioeconomic factors
  - Health status
