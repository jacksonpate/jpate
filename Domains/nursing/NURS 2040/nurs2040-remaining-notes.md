### Course Description
Explores professional nursing care of clients across all stages of human development employing a holistic approach.

### Course Objectives
1. Describe how policy affects healthcare across the lifespan. (Essentials Domains 3, 7, 9) [AUCON Outcomes 2, 6, 7] — Evaluated by: Exam, Class Discussion

## Chapter 23 — Middle-Aged Adult (pp. 597–620)

Source: `Chapter_023+-2040-middle aged.pdf`
Textbook: Edelman & Kudzma, *Health Promotion Throughout the Life Span* (11th ed.)

### Overview
- Age range: 35–65 years
- Significant biological, physiological, and social changes occur
- Expanded responsibilities and increased productivity = sense of accomplishment
- Comprises 40% of US population
- Many changes related to aging and health

---

### Biology and Genetics

**General Body Changes:**
- Thinning, graying hair
- Skin drier, wrinkles, coarsening facial features
- Increased fat disposition, decreased muscle, decreased mass/bone density, increased BMI
- Hormonal changes

**Mortality:**
- Leading causes of death: heart disease, cancer, and accidents
- Morbidity and mortality influenced by lifestyle behaviors

**Genetics — Greater Risk vs. Young Adults:**
- Diabetes mellitus (DM)
- Hypertension (HTN)
- Gout
- Heart disease
- Alcoholism
- Some cancers related to genetics: some breast cancers, colorectal cancers

---

### Healthy People 2030 (Box 23.2, p. 599)
Focus: preventable chronic conditions and behavior modification

Topics:
- Arthritis
- Osteoporosis
- Chronic back conditions
- Diabetes
- Occupational safety and health
- Heart disease
- Stroke

---

### Social Determinants of Health (SDOH)

**Relationship Status and Health:**
- Married adults report better health vs. divorced, widowed, or never-married
- Nearly 60% of US adults are married
- Cohabitation on the rise

**Income Inequality:**
- Greatly impacts health
- Wealth (net worth) is a better health indicator than income
- Major disparities along ethnic lines
  - 2016 Median family wealth: Black = $17,600 | Hispanic = $20,700 | White = $171,000

---

### Gordon's Functional Health Patterns

1. Health perception–health management pattern
2. Nutritional-metabolic pattern
3. Elimination pattern
4. Activity-exercise pattern
5. Sleep-rest pattern
6. Cognitive-perceptual pattern
7. Self-perception–self-concept pattern
8. Roles-relationships pattern
9. Sexuality-reproductive pattern
10. Coping–stress tolerance pattern
11. Values-beliefs pattern

---

### Health Perception–Health Management Pattern

**Self-Destructive Habits:**
- Consequences of smoking, alcohol, overeating surface visibly in middle age

**Health Indicators:**
- Leading cause of death (ages 45–64): heart disease or cancer
- Other causes: accidents, COPD, CVA, DM, Alzheimer's, suicide, flu/pneumonia, liver disease, kidney disease, septicemia

---

### Nutritional-Metabolic Pattern

| Issue | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| High saturated fat diet | 43 million on lipid-lowering drugs for high cholesterol; lifestyle changes + meds |
| Calcium and Vitamin D | Often need to supplement |
| Caffeine use | Common in this age group |
| High sodium diet | Contributes to HTN; avg consumption 4–5 g/day |
| ETOH (alcohol) | Notable concern |

**Oral Health:**
- Dental hygiene essential to overall health
- Risk of gingivitis; oral cancers begin with mucosal changes
- Vulnerable populations: cost is main barrier; tobacco negatively impacts oral health

---

### Elimination Pattern

- Age brings gradual decrease in tone of large intestine
- **Constipation:** sedentary lifestyle, lack of fiber, inadequate fluid intake → dietary fiber preferred over laxatives
- Renal function gradually decreases
- **Incontinence:**
  - Women: risk with multiple births
  - Men: risk as complication of prostate surgery

---

### Activity-Exercise Pattern

- Regular physical activity increases life expectancy and quality of life
- Only a small fraction of adults get enough exercise
- Recommended options: brisk walking, jogging, bicycling, swimming
- **Caution:** hot weather, dehydration, age nearing 65, dizziness, chest pressure, shortness of breath
- Moderation is key; increase activity gradually

---

### Sleep-Rest Pattern

- May get less sleep than young adults due to more demanding lifestyles
- **Insomnia** is a common finding
  - Avoid exercise within 2 hours of bedtime; limit excess caffeine
  - Maintain regular sleep-wake cycle within 24-hour period
  - Cognitive-behavioral therapy is helpful
- **Sleep apnea** is common
  - Associated with MI, CVA, CHF, arrhythmia
- Rest is essential for restorative functions

---

### Cognitive-Perceptual Pattern

**Learning:**
- Learning occurs across the lifespan
- Need to acquire new knowledge or skills persists (employment demands, new life roles)
- Capacity to learn remains constant from ages 35–65
- Reaction time and flexibility may decrease

**Perceptual Changes:**
- **Presbyopia** — farsightedness
- **Glaucoma** — increased intraocular pressure
- Decreased visual sensitivity and peripheral vision
- **Cataracts** — opacity of lens
- **Diabetic retinopathy**
- **Macular degeneration** — deterioration of retina
- **Presbycusis** — loss of higher frequency hearing
- Diminished sense of taste

---

### Self-Perception–Self-Concept Pattern

**Theorists:**
- **Erikson's Theory** — Generativity vs. Stagnation
  - Generativity: sense of productivity, creativity, caring for others
  - Stagnation: lack of accomplishment, self-absorption
- Middle age is a time of critical self-review

**Physical and Psychological Changes:**
- Physiological changes are unavoidable
- Affects self-esteem: gray hair, wrinkles, body changes
- **Women:** menopause and perimenopause → mood swings, anxiety, fatigue, depression
  - Hormonal therapy: limited use after 2002 (WHI study)
  - Alternate products: little scientific data on benefit
- **Men:** also experience physical and psychological reaction to middle age changes
- Tolerance depends on self-satisfaction and level of acceptance

---

### Roles-Relationships Pattern

**Middle Age as Reassessment:**
- Lifestyle choices may have unintended consequences
- Awareness of the inevitability of one's death
- Personal contentment from satisfied choices

**Family Dynamics:**
- **Empty nest syndrome**
- Complex responsibilities: family, work, civic
- Children are "launched" — parents now interact on adult-to-adult level
- Increasing responsibility for aging parents

**Work:**
- Work ethic very important; self-identity tied to work
- More satisfied with employment vs. young adults
- Family and work responsibilities often in conflict
- Traditional husband/wife roles are changing
- **Career clock** is looming: preretirement planning and mid-career changes; many work longer

**Loss and Disruption:**
- **Divorce and separation** — major disruption; effects on children may be long-lasting
- **Death of partner** — profound impact; surviving spouse may be unprepared for single life/parenthood
- Finite nature of life becomes more apparent
- Midlife review is common outcome

---

### Sexuality-Reproductive Pattern

- High proportion of adults remain sexually active
- **Pregnancy:**
  - 30% unintended in women > 35
  - 77% of pregnancies unintended during perimenopause
  - Higher risk of maternal death and fetal complications
- **Physical changes:**
  - Slower arousal
  - Orgasms less intense
  - Longer refractory period between erection and ejaculation (men)
  - Vaginal dryness, decreased libido (women)
- **American cultural value on youth** is challenging for middle-aged women

**Common Issues:**
- **Men:** Erectile dysfunction
- **Women:** Abnormal genital bleeding, secondary amenorrhea

**STIs — Major Public Health Problem:**
- HPV — causes 90% of cervical cancers
- HIV/AIDS — adults > 50 are a "hidden" risk group; less knowledgeable; less likely to discuss sexual behavior with provider

---

### Coping–Stress Tolerance Pattern

**Stress:**
- Unavoidable human experience; promotes negative physiological effects on health
- Mind-body connection can mitigate negative effects
- Techniques: yoga, meditation, tai chi, deep breathing

**Coping with Death of a Parent:**
- Preparation helps feelings of control
- Tools: living will, advanced directive, durable power of attorney

---

### Values-Beliefs Pattern

- Beliefs formed early; often reviewed during crisis
- Crisis often serves as a turning point
- **Committed responsibilities promote moral development:**
  - Valuing others, having relationships, being responsible for others
  - Examples: raising children, mentoring junior employees, serving community

---

### Developmental Theorists

**Erikson & Erikson (1998):**
- Identified *caring* as the middle-aged virtue, often developed at time of crisis

**Kohlberg — Conventional and Post-Conventional Phases:**
Most middle-aged adults are in Stage 3 or 4:
- **Stage 3** — Personal wants and needs (interpersonal)
- **Stage 4** — Duties demanded by society (law and order)
- **Stage 5** — Principles to live by (sacrifice for others)

---

### Hazardous Environmental Issues

**Physical Hazards:**
- **Ionizing radiation** → can cause cancer
- **Water pollution** → cancer and other health issues (benzene, chlordane)
- **Air pollution** → auto emissions, burning fuels, industry
- **Noise pollution** → protective devices required; potentiates hearing loss
- **Occupational hazards** — varied

---

### Hazardous Biological Agents

- Transmitted through air or food/water; enter respiratory or GI tract

**Common Pathogens:**
- **Hepatitis A** — fecal-oral route; poor sanitation
- **Hepatitis B** — blood-borne pathogen; occupational hazard for health professionals
- **Pneumonia** — vaccine available
- **Herpes Zoster (Shingles)** — vaccine available

---

### Chemical Agents

**Workplace/Industrial:**
- Contaminants carried home on clothing; air pollutants
- **Pneumoconiosis** (black lung disease) — coal workers
- **Asbestos** — causes mesothelioma; also affects wood handlers
- Organic carcinogens: benzene, PVC, others

**Tobacco — Leading Cause of Preventable Death:**
- Middle age: may have 30+ pack-year history
- Risks: heart/lung disease, COPD, lung cancer
- 2000+ known chemicals in cigarettes: tar, cyanide, formaldehyde, carbon monoxide

---

### Social Factors and Environment

**Culture and Ethnicity:**
- Must be culturally aware
- Different interpretations of health and illness
- Immigrants: poverty, poor living conditions

**Economics:**
- Net income may be at highest level, but greater financial responsibilities
- Life expectancy increased — many cannot afford to retire in mid-50s to early 60s
- Quality healthcare is expensive — many wait for Medicare before retiring
- Consumer debt and college debt for children are common
- Many supporting children (ages 22–23) still at home
- Health insurance becoming increasingly costly
- Loss of job often means loss of health insurance

---

### Levels of Policy Making — Healthcare Delivery

**Official Agencies (State and Federally Funded):**
- Various state and federal health agencies

**Voluntary Agencies:**
- American Cancer Society
- American Lung Association

**Service Organizations:**
- State Nurses Associations
- YMCA
- American Medical Association
- Tel-Med programs

---

### Nursing Application — Settings Where Nurses Are Active

- **Primary care settings or ER** for middle-aged adults seeking care for themselves or children
- **Home health nurses** after hospital discharge
- **Community health nurses** promoting health in the community
- **Occupational nurses**
- Healthy People 2030 seeks to promote health strategies in a wide variety of settings
- Affordable Care Act expands healthcare access
