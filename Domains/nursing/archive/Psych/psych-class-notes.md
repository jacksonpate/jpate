## Nov 3, 2025 — Psych Exam 3: Social Psychology

**What is Social Psychology?**
- The study of how people influence other's thoughts, feelings, and actions
- How do people understand others?
- How do people work within groups?
- How do people help?

**Social Groups**
- Why do people want to be in a group with others?
  - Resources
  - Protection from threats
  - Control your behavior to fit in-group
- Humans tend to seek group membership

**In-group vs. Out-group**
- **In-group** — frats, sports, team, religion; strong connection
  - In-group favoritism — Reciprocity ("I'll help you, you help me")
- **Out-group** — groups that you're not a part of
  - Out-group homogeneity effect — view out-group members as more alike
- **Social identity:** we define ourselves partly through our group affiliations; take pride in accomplishments of the group

**Tajfel & Turner (1979)** — studied in-group/out-group behavior

---

**Group Influences**

**Social Facilitation** — The presence of others generally enhances individual performance (opposites apply)

**Social Loafing** — People may work less hard with a group than on their own
- Group project → shared goal → lack of personal responsibility
- "If I put in less effort → they will pick up more"

**Group Polarization** — The initial attitudes of groups become more extreme over time
- If most group members are willing to take risks → whole group becomes risky
- Even if some individual members don't take risks

**Conformity** — Changing one's behavior in order to match other's behavior
- Informative → following others over your behavior ("They know something you don't")
- Normative → following others' behaviors to fit in
- **Asch (1955)** → Study on conformity

Why do people conform? → Might get kicked out of group
- **Schachter (1951)** — When a dissenter goes against group = ostracized

**11/5/25 Wednesday — Obedience to Authority**

- Pivotal work: **Milgram (1960s)** → famous study
- **Milgram's Experiment** — Learner = confederate; actor with the study
  - Participants told they will be a "teacher" in a memory task
  - When learner makes error they will be shocked
  - Learner has heart problem
  - Machine capable from 15-450 volts
    - 75 volts — learner cries in pain
    - 150 volts — learner screaming, bangs on wall, demands to be let out
    - 300 volts — learner refuses to answer question
    - >330 volts — learner goes silent
  - If participant tries to stop, experimenter tells them to continue
  - **65% of participants were obedient (2/3)**
  - Almost all participants tried to stop
  - But 2/3 of participants delivered shock all the way to 450 volts

**Bystander Intervention**
- People often act to help others who are in need
- Altruism — providing help when needed, even without reward
- But social context can influence how likely people are to help
- Research into this effect prompted by murder of **Kitty Genovese**
  - Attacked for half an hour; 38 eye-witnesses, none tried to help or called police

- **Latané and Darley (1968):** A person is less likely to help if there are others around
  - Experiment: If participant is alone → more likely to help
  - If they are with confederates → likely not
  - **The more people, the less likely for action**
  - **Diffusion of Responsibility** → expect others to help; no one feels personally responsible
  - The more others are present, the less responsible one feels
  - Social Blunders (Fear of) — social mistake

- Anonymity → less likely to help if you are anonymous
- Weighing of costs and benefits
  - "What will the cost be to me of helping?"
  - E.g., only 10% of Samaritans who were late for a lecture they were giving stopped to help a man who was slumped over; if not rushing, 63% stopped to help

**11/12/25 — Psych → Disorders**

**Anxiety Disorders** → Can interfere with sleep, attention, concentration
- **Phobias** → fear of specific thing → cause of proportion (often associated with other disorders/depression)
- **Social Anxiety** → fear of being negatively viewed by others; meeting new people, fear of public speaking
- **Generalized Anxiety** → Anxiety that's diffuse (~6% of Americans) → Causes: fatigue, irritability, sleep problems, headaches, restlessness
- **Panic Disorder** → sudden terror attacks accompanied by worry ("comes out of nowhere") → sweating, trembling, increased heart rate, chest pain, dizziness, numbness/tingling in hands/feet
- Physiological arousal leads to body symptoms → leads to restless behavior (rapid heart rate, sweating, dry mouth, increased blood pressure, fidgeting, exaggerated startle)

**OCD — Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder**
- Unwanted thoughts that lead to emotional distress and anxiety
- Doing maladaptive behaviors to reduce anxiety
- Fear of germs = wash hands
- Affects 1-2% of population
- Typically begins in early adulthood
- Common topics: contamination, accidents

**Depressive Disorders (Depression)**
- Different from just being sad
- Sad, empty, irritable feelings that persist over time
- Interferes with daily functioning
- Accompanied by symptoms and cognitive problems
- Diagnosis: must have experienced major depressive episode; depressed mood or loss of pleasure in pleasurable activities every day
- Very common — 16% of Americans; "The Common Cold" of psychological disorders
