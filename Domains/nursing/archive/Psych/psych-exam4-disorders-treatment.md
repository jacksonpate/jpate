# Psych Exam 4 — Disorders, Treatment & Social Psych Review

## Kahoot Review: Key Concepts

**Beck's Cognitive Triad** — Negative view of:
- Self
- World
- Future

**Bipolar Disorder** — Characterized by cycles of **Mania + Bad Mood**

**Learned Helplessness** — Someone who believes they can do **nothing** to stop bad things happening

**Hallucinations** = False perceptions
**Delusions** = False beliefs

---

## Schizophrenia

**Negative Symptoms:**
- Lack of emotion
- Apathy
- Slowed speech

**Anti-psychotic medications** — Essential for treatment of schizophrenia

---

## Personality Disorders

**Borderline Personality Disorder**
- Intense fear of being abandoned
- Linked to early sexual abuse

**Anti-Social Personality Disorder**
- Linked to early malnutrition
- Typically show no remorse when their actions harm others
- **No effective treatments to help**
- Most effective reduction: the passage of time (aging)

**Autism Spectrum Disorder**
- Typically does **NOT** have excessive anxiety
- Increased diagnoses in last few decades → reflects improvements in diagnosis

---

## Psychological Disorders

**Important Criterion for Diagnosing** → Person's thoughts or behaviors are **interfering with life activities**

**Maladaptive behavior** — If it interferes with daily life activities

**DSM** — Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders

**Co-Morbid** → Two disorders that tend to occur together (Comorbidity)

**Etiology** → The underlying cause of a psychological disorder

**Diathesis-Stress Model** → Results from an underlying predisposition and an environmental trigger

**Externalizing disorders** — More common in men; characterized by behavioral disinhibition

---

## Anxiety Disorders

**Phobia** → Fear of specific object or situation → Anxiety Disorder
- **Social Anxiety Disorder** → Fear of being negatively evaluated by others
- **Panic Disorder** → Sudden attacks of intense fear with no obvious trigger
- **Generalized Anxiety Disorder** → Anxious most of the time, but doesn't know what about

**OCD** — Someone with OCD carries out compulsive behaviors to reduce anxiety
- From intrusive thoughts
- A **Compulsion** = behavior carried out to reduce anxiety from obsessive thoughts

**Depressive Disorder:**
- Feeling of emptiness
- Physical symptoms (fatigue)
- Distorted, maladaptive thoughts

**ADHD** → Characterized by impulsivity, restlessness, difficulty concentrating

---

## Treatments

| Treatment | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Psychoanalysis** (Freud) | Make people aware of contents in unconscious mind |
| **Behavior Therapy** | Relies on principles of conditioning |
| **Exposure Therapy** | Example of behavioral therapy |
| **Cognitive Restructuring** | Replacing distorted thoughts with realistic thoughts |
| **Cognitive Behavior Therapy (CBT)** | Particularly effective in treating depressive disorder |
| **Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT)** | Delivers electrical current; affects most of brain; used to treat depression |
| **Deep Brain Stimulation** | Surgically implanted electrodes to stimulate brain activity |
| **Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation** | Stimulates brain activity |

**Freud's purpose of psychotherapy** → Make a patient aware of unconscious mental conflicts

**Cognitive therapy beneficial use** → Changing self-defeating thoughts contributing to depression

**Depressive disorder treatment options:** All of the following work:
- Psychopharmaceutical medications
- Cognitive Behavior Therapy
- Transcranial magnetic stimulation

**Psychotropic medications** — Work by affecting **neurotransmitter levels**

| Practitioner | Role |
|-------------|------|
| **Psychiatrist** | Can prescribe psychotropic medications |
| **Counseling Psychologist** | Helps people cope with difficult life circumstances (not significant mental illness) |

5. Presence of others enhances performance → **Social Facilitation**

9. Underlying cause of a disorder → **Etiology**
10. Externalizing disorders → more common in men
11. Mark anxious but unsure why → **Generalized Anxiety Disorder**
12. Frieda's model (genetic predisposition + environmental trigger) → **Diathesis-Stress Model**
13. Learned Helplessness = person believes unable to control events; pointless to try

## Exam 4 Quiz — Stereotypes & Social Cognition

12. Key reason stereotypes persist → **We pay attention to information that confirms stereotypes**
13. **Prejudice** = Thoughts; **Discrimination** = Behavior
14. Favorable attitudes from repeated encounters → **Mere Exposure Effect**
15. "Ohio State fans are all the same" → **Outgroup Homogeneity Effect**

**Out-Groups** = Social groups which one doesn't belong to
**Reciprocity** → The understanding that I will help you and you will help me, as needed
**Implicit Attitudes** → Opinions, beliefs, or feelings that one is **not aware of** but can influence behavior

**Reasons bystanders fail to intervene:**
- Diffusion of Responsibility
- Cost-Benefit analysis
- Fear of making a Social Blunder
- (Religiosity is **NOT** a reason)
