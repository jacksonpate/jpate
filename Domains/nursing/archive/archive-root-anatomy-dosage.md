## BIOL 2510 Exam 2 Study Guide

### Respiratory System
- **Boyle's Law**: pressure and volume are inversely related — basis of breathing mechanics
- **Spirometry** measures lung volumes: tidal volume, inspiratory/expiratory reserve, residual volume
- **Hemoglobin**: carries O2 in RBCs; O2 binding affected by pH, temperature, CO2 (Bohr effect)
- Breathing muscles: diaphragm (primary), external intercostals (inspiration); internal intercostals, abdominals (forced expiration)

### Digestive System
- **Stomach cells**: chief cells (pepsinogen), parietal cells (HCl + intrinsic factor), G cells (gastrin), mucous cells
- **GI phases**: cephalic (anticipation), gastric (food in stomach), intestinal (food in small intestine)
- **Liver functions**: bile production, detox, glycogen storage, plasma protein synthesis
- **Pancreas (exocrine)**: secretes amylase, lipase, proteases into duodenum via pancreatic duct

## BIOL 2510 Exam 3 (Part II) Study Guide

### Endocrine System
- **Hormone types**: amino acid-based (water-soluble, surface receptors) vs steroid (lipid-soluble, nuclear receptors)
- **Hypothalamus-pituitary axis**: hypothalamus releases releasing/inhibiting hormones → anterior pituitary responds
- **Growth Hormone (GH)**: stimulates IGF-1 from liver; promotes growth of bone and muscle
- **Thyroid hormones (T3/T4)**: increase metabolic rate; require iodine; controlled by TSH
- **Cortisol**: glucocorticoid from adrenal cortex; released under stress; raises blood glucose, suppresses immune
- **Insulin**: released by beta cells (pancreas) when blood glucose high → promotes glucose uptake
- **Glucagon**: released by alpha cells (pancreas) when blood glucose low → promotes glycogenolysis

### Type 1 vs Type 2 Diabetes
| | Type 1 | Type 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Cause | Autoimmune — beta cells destroyed | Insulin resistance |
| Onset | Usually childhood | Usually adult |
| Treatment | Insulin required | Lifestyle, oral meds, insulin if needed |

## BIOL 2510 Exam 4 Part 1 — Lymphatic & Reproductive Systems

### Lymphatic System
- **Lymphedema**: swelling from blocked/damaged lymphatic vessels → fluid accumulates in tissue
- **Lymphadenopathy**: enlarged lymph nodes — sign of infection or malignancy
- Lymph nodes filter lymph; spleen filters blood; thymus matures T-cells

### Reproductive System — Cell Division
- **Mitosis**: somatic cells; 2 identical daughter cells (diploid); no genetic variation
- **Meiosis**: gametes only; 4 haploid daughter cells; crossing over → genetic variation

### Ovarian Cycle (3 phases)
1. **Follicular phase**: FSH stimulates follicle development; estrogen rises
2. **Ovulation**: LH surge → follicle ruptures, egg released (~day 14)
3. **Luteal phase**: corpus luteum forms → secretes progesterone; if no fertilization, corpus luteum degenerates

### Spermatogenesis
- Occurs in seminiferous tubules; regulated by FSH and testosterone
- Spermatogonia → primary spermatocytes → secondary spermatocytes → spermatids → spermatozoa
- **Varicocele**: enlarged veins in scrotum → raises testicular temperature → impairs sperm production; leading cause of male infertility

## Urinary System — Study Notes (Exam 3)

### System Overview
- Components: kidneys, ureters, urinary bladder, urethra
- Ureters transport waste from kidneys to bladder
- Bladder holds ~4 cups (2 pints) of urine; urethra eliminates urine

### Kidney Functions
- Filter blood → convert filtrate to urine → remove waste (urea, uric acid)
- Control blood ion balance: sodium, potassium, calcium, phosphate ions
- Regulate acid-base balance: alter hydrogen and bicarbonate ion levels
- Regulate blood pressure: kidneys release **renin** → hormone cascade changes BP
- Regulate blood volume: excretion of urine controls volume
- **Calcitriol** (hormone produced by kidneys): increases calcium absorption from small intestine → increases blood calcium concentration
- **Erythropoietin (EPO)**: produced and released when low blood oxygen → increases rate of erythrocyte release from bone marrow
- Potential to engage **gluconeogenesis** (glucose from non-carbs) during fasting/starvation
- Regulate erythrocytes; monitor glucose (affected by starvation and fasting)

### Kidney Anatomy
- Bean-shaped; symmetrical; concave medial border
- Shape: C/D shape — surface (top), bean shape overall
- **Medial border → Renal Hilum**: where blood vessels, nerves enter; beginning of ureter
- Right kidney is lower and smaller (to accommodate liver)
- **Kidney parenchyma**: Renal Cortex (outer) + Renal Medulla (inner)
- External tissue of kidney = **Fibrous Capsule** (maintains shape and protection)
- **Renal Fascia**: anchors kidney; white paranephric fat cushions
- **Perirenal fat**: provides support and stabilization
- Loss of adipose connective tissue → can lead to **Renal Ptosis** and ureter obstruction

4. **Nephron** = functional filtration unit of kidney
8. **Corticomedullary Junction**: wide base of renal pyramids at junction between cortex and medulla
9. **Columns in kidney anatomy**: extensions of the cortex into the medulla
10. Kidneys regulate ion levels by eliminating them
13. **Renal Corpuscle** = Glomerulus + Bowman's (glomerular) capsule; glomerulus is a tuft of capillary loops where blood enters for filtration
16. **Renal Papilla**: medially directed tip of pyramid
17. **Minor + Major Calyces**: part of urine drainage system
18. **Capsular space of glomerular capsule**: receives and begins modification of filtrate
19. **Vascular pole**: arterioles attach to glomerulus
20. Right kidney lower + smaller to accommodate liver
21. Fibrous capsule maintains kidney shape and protection

- Almost all nephrons located in cortex

## Dosages & Calculations — Safe Medication Administration

### The 9 Rights
1. Right Patient
2. Right Medication
3. Right Route
4. Right Time
5. Right Dose
6. Right Assessment
7. Right to Refuse
8. Right Education
9. Right Evaluation

### Prescription Types
| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Routine** | Given on a regular schedule until discontinued |
| **Standing** | Pre-authorized for specific conditions/circumstances |
| **Single One-Time** | Given once only |
| **Start** | Must be given within **30 minutes** of order |
| **PRN** | As needed — given when patient requests or condition requires |
| **Urgent/Now** | Given within **30 min to 1 hour** of order |

### Prescription Components (required elements)
- Date and time written
- Patient name
- Medication name
- Indication (reason)
- Dose
- Route of administration
- Dispensing instructions
- Provider signature
