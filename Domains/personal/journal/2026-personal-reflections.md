# What Jackson Has Shared Personally

## Identity & The Real Fear
- Biggest fear isn't wasted potential — it's **not being good enough**. The deeper fear is trying everything and still not being enough.
- Biggest lie he tells himself: *"I should already have it figured out."*
- Sees everyone as better than him. That's the internal story nobody sees.
- Keeps people at a distance because he's scared if they see the real him they won't like what they find.
- Feels like a side character in everyone else's story — but wants desperately to matter in someone's life.
- Nobody fully gets how much he's in his head second-guessing everything he does and says.

## Social Modes
Two distinct modes — both real, both Jackson:
- **Locked-in mode:** studying, building, goal-oriented, structured, mentally serious
- **Letting loose mode:** out with the boys, turns off the seriousness, enjoys it fully, rarely regrets it

The ability to switch between them is intentional. "That's the time to let loose." Wakes up after going out with neutral-to-good feelings — no guilt, just memories of how fun it was. Doesn't escape into going out, just enjoys it.

## Money

## Alcohol
Treats it socially, not as an escape. Goes out with friends, has fun, doesn't black out or do things he regrets. Part of normal social life, not a crutch.
