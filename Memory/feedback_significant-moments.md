---
name: Significant Moments documentation rule
description: How to document meaningful conversations and moments in People pages — situation summaries, not quote logs
type: feedback
---

When a conversation or message thread contains something meaningful — emotional weight, conflict, a turning point, something that changed the relationship, something that reveals who that person is to Jackson — do NOT summarize what they said.

Document the **situation**: what was happening, what was said between them, what the context was, what it meant, and how it landed. Write it as a situation summary.

**Where it goes:** That person's People page, under a section called `## Significant Moments`.

**Format:** If there are multiple moments, each gets its own subsection with a date and a short title that captures what the moment was about.

```
## Significant Moments

### [Date] — [Short title]
[Situation summary: context, what was said, what it meant, how it landed]
```

**Why:** A quote log without context is noise. The point is to preserve what actually happened and what it *meant* — so any future session has real understanding, not just a transcript.

**How to apply:** Any time Jackson shares a conversation, message thread, or moment that has emotional or relational significance. Also apply retroactively when enriching People pages if source material contains meaningful moments.
