---
name: Third party conversation rule
description: When Jackson and someone else discuss a third person, that third person's page gets updated too — not just the page of whoever he was texting
type: feedback
---

If Jackson and another person are texting about someone else — a third person who isn't in the conversation — that information belongs on that third person's page too, not just the page of whoever Jackson was texting.

For every conversation:
- Identify every person mentioned, talked about, or referenced — not just who Jackson was talking to
- Any meaningful context about that third person goes on their People page under a section called `## Mentioned In` — with a note on who was talking about them, when, and what was said about them
- Backlink that entry back to the conversation it came from and to the page of whoever Jackson was texting at the time

**Format:**
```
## Mentioned In

### [Date] — mentioned by [[Person]] in conversation with Jackson
[What was said about this person, what it revealed, why it matters]
Source: [[People/Person]], [[Personal Data/Snapchat/Messages/username]]
```

**Example:** Jackson and Trey texting about Emma → Emma's page gets a ## Mentioned In entry with what was said about her, backlinked to [[Trey Frachiseur]] and the Trey conversation file. Trey's page also notes the conversation referenced Emma.

**Why:** Third-party information is often the most revealing context about a person — how others talk about them when they're not there. That information shouldn't be buried in the wrong page.

**How to apply:** Any time processing conversations or message threads. This runs alongside the Significant Moments rule — they work together.
