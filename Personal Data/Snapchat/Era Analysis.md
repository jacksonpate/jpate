## The 1,666 Deleted Friends

The number is striking: 1,666 deleted friends against 464 current friends. Jackson shed 3.6× his current network.

This isn't attrition — it's a reset. High school social graphs tend to be geographically concentrated (same school, same town). Auburn brought a new population, new context, and new identity. Most high school contacts that weren't carried forward actively were eventually deleted.

What survived the cut:
- Relationships with strong individual bonds that transcended geography (atallent97, trey_frachiseur, mrbradyt07)
- People who also ended up at Auburn or in Jackson's new world
- Anyone with enough staying power to remain active through the transition

The 1,666 represents everyone else: the acquaintances, the faded friendships, the people who were proximity-based and not bond-based. The high school → Auburn transition is the biggest social graph event in the dataset.
